About

I'm a third-year BSc Computer Science student who got into tech the real way by actually doing it. I built my own PC from scratch, switched my laptop from Windows 10 to Linux, messed around with Arch and Kali and daily drive Ubuntu on top of a Windows dual-boot. I don't just study my passion. I thrive on it.

Computers have been part of my life for as long as I can remember. My uncle got me into them early he taught me a lot, sparked the curiosity and showed me what was possible. And my late grandfather was a computer nerd himself, someone who did software development back in the day. The fact that I'm now walking the same path means a lot to me. It feels like something that runs in the family.

I'm also a student representative for my entire BSc intake sitting in meetings with deans and university officials, advocating for students and making sure problems actually get solved. Outside of all that, I'm into car culture I go to meets regularly with friends. I like reading and getting lost in a good book and gaming is a big part of how I unwind. I go all-in on the things I care about.

My goal is to work in big tech solving real problems at scale, building things that matter, growing into someone who truly knows their craft. I'm only at the start of that journey and I'm not slowing down.

Why a Website?

I'm applying for jobs with a Computer Science degree and no formal work experience yet. A standard PDF CV can list what I've studied but it can't show what I can actually do. So instead of handing over a document, I built one.

This website is the application. Every line of it the layout, the animations, the custom cursor, the scroll reveal logic, the responsive design was written by hand, from scratch. It's not a template. It's proof.

I wanted whoever's reading this to see that I don't just know the theory. I can sit down, figure something out and ship it. That's the kind of person I am and a PDF couldn't really show you that.

The Problem
No work experience to list. A traditional CV would look empty next to candidates who've already had jobs.
The Solution
Build something. Let the work speak. This site is the portfolio and the CV at the same time.
What it shows
Frontend development, attention to detail, design thinking and the ability to take an idea and execute it without being told how.
The bigger point
I'm someone who solves problems instead of waiting for permission. That's the kind of person you want on your team.
